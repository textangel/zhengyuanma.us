Multi-Armed Bandits

Basic k-armed bandit

  • Each arm a
    • Wins (reward=1) with fixed (unknown) prob μa
    • Loses (reward=0) with fixed (unknown) prob 1−μa
  • All draws are independent given μ1,...,μk
  • Problem: How to pull arms to maximize total rewards?

Stochastic k-armed bandit

Setting

  • Set of k choices (arms)
  • Each choice a is associated with unknown probability distribution Pa with support in [0,1]. Given Pa, rewards are independent between draws.
  • In each round t:
    • We pick some arm a and obtain as reward random sample Xt from Pa.
  • Our goal is to maximize ∑Tt=1Xt

Solution

This is a RL algorithm. We find a policy: a strategy or rule that in each iteration tells me which arm to pull.
We quantify performance of the algo using regret.
Def: Regret:

  • Let μa be the mean of Pa
  • The payoff/reward of the best arm is μ∗=maxaμa
  • Let i1,i2,...,ir be the sequence of arms pulled
  • Instantaneous regret at time t: rt=μ∗−μat
  • Total regret is RT=∑Tt=1rt
  • A typical goal is: We wnat a policy (arm allocation strategy) that guarentees RTT→0 as T→∞
