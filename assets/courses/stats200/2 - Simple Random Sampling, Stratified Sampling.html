Survey Sampling

Recall in the population case, μ=1N∑Ni=1Xi, σ2=1N∑Ni=1(Xi−μ)2.
Suppose we are sampling from a population of size N which consists of groups of values ξk,j=1,...,m<N, and each ξk is repeated nk times in the population.

Then μ=1N∑Ni=1Xi=1N∑mi=1nkξk
And σ2=1N∑Ni=1(Xi−μ)2
=1N∑mi=1nk(ξk−μ)2
=1N∑mi=1nkξ2k−μ2

The sampling distribution is the probability distribution of the sample (the mean of the sample, the variance of the sample, etc).
Generally, x1,...,xN denotes points in the population (lowercase x, capital N), and Xi,...,Xn (capital X, lowercase n) denote sampled points.

Simple Random Sampling

From dataset x1,...,xN, divided into groups ξk,j=1,...,m as above. Choose n samples X1,...,Xn without replacement such that P(Xi=xj)=1N for all i,j. (alternately, P(Xi=ξk)=nk/N).

Sample Mean Statistics

X¯n:=μ̂ :=1n∑ni=1Xi
E[μ̂ ]=μ, and Var(μ̂ )=σ2nN−nN−1.
Thus, X¯n∼N(μ,σ2nN−nN−1).
Note 1. When Xi is an indicator variable centered at p we have Xn¯∼N(p,p(1−p)nN−nN−1)
Note 2. We often use Var(μ̂ )=σ2nN−nN as a slight of hand.
The correction N−nN−1 is called tbe finite population correction. n/N is called the sampling fraction.
Sample Mean Expectation
E[μ̂ ]=1n∑jE[Xj]. Since for each j, E[Xj]=1N∑mi=1nkξk=μ, E[μ̂ ]=μ overall. Thus the sample mean is unbiased.

Stratified Sampling

Cut up the data into logical groups (people by demographics, business deals by location, etc). Independently sample with out replacement from within each strata.
Benefits: If the characteristics within each grouping or statum is rather stable, we can estimate population mean and variance with fewer data points, because using stata gives you a reduction in variance.

Suppose our dataset is divided into L (l=1,...,L) strata each with Nl elements, and call the weight Nl/N of each stratum wl. What is the mean and variance of the stratified sampled mean?

Define X¯strat=∑Ll=1NlNXl¯=∑Ll=1wlXl¯, where we define X¯l=1nl∑nli=1xil

  • μ̂ :E[X¯strat]=∑Ll=1wlE[Xl¯]=∑Ll=1wlXl¯=μ
  • σ̂ 2:Var(X¯strat)=∑Ll=1w2lNl−nlNl−1σ2nnl by below:
    Var(X¯strat)=Var(∑Ll=1wlXl¯)=∑Ll=1w2lVar(Xl¯)+0=∑Ll=1w2lNl−nlNl−1σ2nnl
    (Above, there is no covariance term because we take an independent sample across the strata.)
