Intro

I graduated from Stanford in Computer Science and Mathematics in 2017. At Stanford, my coursework mostly focused on Computer Science from a statistical angle. In addition to the core coursework in computer systems, data structures, computational complexity, algorithms, and machine learning, I took classes in computational theory, statistical inference, ANOVA, and optimisation. I also spent some time in the Econs department, looking at mathematical foundations of economics, taking Game Theory with Prof. Gabriel Caroll and 1st Year PhD Microeconomics.

My main technical interests are in Natural Language Processing and Recommendation Systems. In my previous work experience I worked on PySpark pipelines for promotion personalization, machine learning models for revenue prediction, and product recommendation models for retail clients. In my free time I enjoy working on mathematical and algorithmic puzzles.

In addition to this, I studied abroad for two years in China on a master's scholarship and am the record-holder for the USA Mathematical Olympiad for the State of Hawaii.

Skills

I am a skilled Python developer, having worked for several years in the language. I also have signficant experience with the distributed computing stack, mostly through Spark (PySpark), with exposure to Scala. I have experience using both RDD and SparkSQL APIs, Spark ETL on large datasets, and parallel Machine Learning using Spark libraries.

As for the rest of the stack, I have worked with HDFS, Amazon S3, Hive, and Kubernetes. In terms of machine learning frameworks, I have experience in numerical programmning and deep learning using both PyTorch and Tensorflow, and in particular have some focused experience in Natural Language Processing.

In addition to this, I have a strong understanding of optimization, data mining, traditional ML, and deep learning algorithms in their applications to information retrieval, personalization, NLP and Computer Vision. I have also developed minor applications in JavaScript and C#.

Work

                               

Most recently, I worked as a Data Scientist at Grab, South-East Asia's largest tech unicorn. I worked on Python and PySpark data pipelining and model training for a promotion-targeting system that covered over a third of the company's largest market. Along the way I introduced optimizations that gave us a 10x code speedup, which allowed us to attain our final scale.

Prior to Grab, I was completing my masters, during which time I interned at AInnovation, an AI-Enterprise startup owned by Kai-Fu Lee and spun out of his venture fund, Sinovation Ventures. During my internship I wrote two recommender system apps for two retail clients.

Prior to this, I interned at proprietary trading firm Gelber Group's options trading desk, where I built two apps (mostly in C#) that made it into the trading software. The first app computed real-time portfolio collateral for the traders, and the second app computed profit attributions to individual traders, which the options director used to allocate bonuses.

In addition to the above working experiences, I have also performed statistical research with the Stanford Economics Department and completed internships in Data Engineering at a Chinese ERP Startup and a Digital Currency Startup.
